Each patient has their own unique needs. The best way to determine a problem, and also to make a plan for a treatment and care of your teeth is to consult with a dentist or specialist in prosthetics.

When do you need a treatmnet from a specialist in prothetics?

Treatment by a specialist is needed in some of the following situations: when missing one or more teeth, in need of a removable denture or partial denture, when a dental crown od a bridge is necessary and of course when you want to improve the esthetics of your teeth.

In such cases, the dentists can offer you long lasting solutions for your teeth: dental crowns, dental bridges, mobile dentures and full or partial dentures as well. In generally, any dental treatment as previously mentiones is called full mouth reconstruction. These treatments can be changed depending by the patient's needs, but above all - by the patient's will.

What causes tooth loss?

Generally, tooth loss can occur as a result of advanced tooth decay, injury, disease, periodontitis (advanced gum disease), even of trauma. Teeth loss generates functional and esthetic problems, as well losing your confidence. Prosthetics specialists are qualified to restore your smile and the optimal function of your teeth. Regular dentist visits and monitoring are recommended in order to avoid this unpleasent appearance.

I have sensitive teeth that easily bleed. Is there anything I can do about it?

There are several solutions and recommendations for sensitive teeth. The most recognized is the use of toothpaste for sensitive teeth and gums. Sensitivity toothpaste, which contains strontium chloride or potassium nitrate are very effective in treating sensitive teeth. In most cases, just after a few weeks of use the patients may notice a decrease in sensitivity. Highly acidic foods such as oranges, grapefruits and lemons, as well as tea and soda can increase tooth sensitivity and work against any sensitivity toothpaste, that's why a reduced consumption of the same products is recommended .

High-fluoride containing care products can also be recommended to help reduce tooth sensitivity. Dentist or prosthetics specialists can help you to get rid of this unpleasent presence.

What causes poor and unpleasant breath?

There are several causes of bad breath. The most common ones are: dry mouth, tobacco products, oral infection, health problems and poor quality of dental hygiene.

Maintaining good oral hygiene is essential for reducing the bad breath. This can be achieved by regular dentist visits. Also, it is recommended brushing your teeth twice a day, and if needed - use floss threader. If your dentist determines that your teeth are healty, but you still have the problem, it is advised to visit your family doctor, so the cause would be quickly discovered and removed.

I’ve recently been diagnosed with diabetes. Are there any dental problems that are associated with this disease and how can they affect my health?

Infections and other problems such as receding gums and periodontal disease are common afflictions among diabetics. Diabetes impairs the body’s ability to fight off infections and results with decreasing blood flow. These factors tend to promote this kinds of problems to the patients.

Good oral hygiene, proper brushing and regular visits to the dentist can help prevent and counteract with this kind of desease.

As I entry into pregnancy, my gums are more sensitive and they easily bleed. Is there any and what is the relation between the pregnancy and the dentistry?

Changing hormone levels during pregnancy can cause normal, healthy gums to become more irritated, inflamed and swollen. This irritation, known as “Pregnancy Gingivitis” is the body’s reaction durng pregnancy.

A regular visit to the dentist during pregnancy is particularly important for an early detection and prevention of potential infections and inflammation of the gums. It is usual that bigger and more serious problems or treatments should be delayed after the pregnancy, but for the urgent cases is recommended to be solved and cured as soon as possible. Because many of the baby’s organs are being formed in the first trimester, the ideally time to be taken care of is during the second trimester to minimize any potential risk.
